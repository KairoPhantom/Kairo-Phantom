BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers for Language Understanding
Jacob Devlin, Ming-Wei Chang, Kenton Lee, Kristina Toutanova

Abstract:
We introduce a new language representation model called BERT, which stands for Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers. Unlike recent language representation models, BERT is designed to pre-train deep bidirectional representations from unlabeled text by jointly conditioning on both left and right context in all layers.

Key Claims:
1. BERT achieves state-of-the-art performance on 11 NLP tasks.
2. Pre-training deep bidirectional representations improves task performance.

Methods:
BERT uses the Transformer encoder architecture with 12 layers, 768 hidden units, and 12 self-attention heads. The model is pre-trained on two unsupervised tasks: Masked Language Model and Next Sentence Prediction.

Results:
BERT achieves 80.5 F1 on SQuAD v1.1 and 84.1 F1 on SQuAD v2.0.
