# Community Seeding Plan — Pre-Launch

> **Principle**: Community is built through sustained presence, not a single post. 
> "Stars ≠ traction. Active contributors > passive stars."

## Pre-Launch Timeline (3 weeks before Show HN)

### Week -3: Foundation
- Create r/KairoPhantom subreddit
- Post 3 tutorials:
  1. "How to use Kairo Phantom with paperless-ngx for local document AI"
  2. "Setting up Ollama + Qwen3 for offline document processing with Kairo"
  3. "Provenance receipts: how Kairo shows its work (and why cloud AI can't)"

### Week -2: r/selfhosted
- Post a TUTORIAL (not a launch ad): "I built a local-first document AI that works with paperless-ngx"
- Position: "Kairo is the AI brain; paperless-ngx is the archive"
- Engage with comments for 48+ hours (sustained presence)

### Week -1: r/LocalLLaMA
- Post: "Running Qwen3 locally with Kairo for offline document processing"
- Focus on the local LLM angle (no cloud, no API keys)
- Share benchmarks (200-page contract processing time on CPU)

### Week 0: Show HN
- 60-second demo: upload contract → extract clauses → provenance receipt → redlined document
- Title: "Kairo Phantom — Local-first document AI that shows its work (provenance receipts)"
- Be present in comments for 24+ hours

## Discord Server
- Channels: #general, #help, #self-hosted, #legal-use-cases, #dev-log, #contributors
- Target: 100 members week 1, 500 members month 1

## What Community Seeding Actually Requires
1. **Sustained presence** — not just posting, but answering questions for weeks
2. **Real tutorials** — not marketing, but genuine "how I solved X" content
3. **Paperless-ngx bridge** — entry point to r/selfhosted community
4. **Provenance receipts** — the differentiator that cloud AI cannot match
5. **Honest expectations** — 100 Discord members week 1, not 10,000

## What NOT to Do
- Don't post launch ads disguised as tutorials
- Don't promise features that don't exist yet
- Don't compare to OpenClaw (different product, different audience)
- Don't expect Product Hunt to drive traction (487/500 dead)

## Success Metrics (Honest)
- Week 1: 100 Discord members, 500-2000 stars
- Month 1: 500 Discord members, 5-10 active contributors
- Month 6: 50 active contributors (the real success metric)
