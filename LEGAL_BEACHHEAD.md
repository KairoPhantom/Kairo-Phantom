## LB-2: Bar Association Compliance Artifact

### "Will the Bar Allow This?"

**One-Page PDF for Compliance Teams**

**Q: Does using Kairo Phantom create an unauthorized practice of law issue?**

A: No. Kairo is a drafting assistant, not a legal advisor. It generates text that the attorney reviews and approves before insertion. The attorney exercises independent professional judgment on every output. Kairo is analogous to a sophisticated spell-checker or a junior associate's first draft — the professional remains responsible.

**Q: Does Kairo access, store, or transmit client data?**

A: Never. Kairo Phantom operates 100% locally:
- All AI inference runs on the local machine (Ollama + local LLM)
- No data is sent to any external server
- MemMachine stores preferences in a local SQLite database at `~/.kairo-phantom/`
- Network calls: zero. Verified by examining `phantom-core/src/ai.rs` — all endpoints are `localhost:11434`

**Q: Does Kairo comply with ABA Model Rule 1.6 (Confidentiality)?**

A: Yes. Since no client data leaves the machine, there is no disclosure of client information to a third party. The software is equivalent to a local word processor. 

ABA Formal Opinion 477R (2017): Lawyers must take reasonable measures to prevent inadvertent disclosure when using technology. Kairo's architecture (100% local) meets the highest standard — no transmission, no disclosure risk.

**Q: What about state bar rules on AI disclosure?**

A: Several states (California, New York, Florida) have issued guidance requiring disclosure of AI use in some contexts. Kairo's output is always reviewed and approved by the attorney before use — the same standard as reviewing a paralegal's work. Disclosure requirements typically apply to AI making autonomous legal determinations, not drafting assistance reviewed by a licensed attorney.

**Recommendation:** Include a standard AI-assistance disclosure in engagement letters:  
*"Our firm may use AI-assisted drafting tools. All AI-generated content is reviewed and approved by a licensed attorney before use."*
